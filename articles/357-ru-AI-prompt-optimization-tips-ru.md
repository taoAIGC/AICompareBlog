Many people ask me: Why can others write viral content with ChatGPT while you only get generic responses?

The answer is simple: **If you don't know how to ask, AI won't know how to answer.**

Prompt is the translator between you and AI. The clearer and more specific your instructions, the more accurate AI's responses.

Today I'm sharing 5 tested prompt optimization techniques that work with ChatGPT, Claude, and DeepSeek.

## 1. Role-Playing Method: Give AI an Identity

**Wrong:**
> "Write an article about marketing"

**Right:**
> "You are an internet marketing expert with 10 years of experience, specializing in user growth and brand strategy. Please write an article about private domain traffic operation..."

**Why it works:**
AI adjusts its response style, depth, and focus based on the role. A clear identity helps AI call upon relevant expertise.

## 2. Task Breakdown: Don't Ask AI to Do Too Much at Once

**Wrong:**
> "Help me write a 2000-word in-depth analysis"

**Correct:**
> "Step 1: Analyze three major development trends in online education
> Step 2: Predict competitive strategies of top players
> Step 3: Give 3 differentiation suggestions for small businesses"

**Why it works:**
AI performs better on single tasks than complex ones. After splitting, each step has a clear goal.

## 3. Example-Guided Method: Give AI a Reference

**Wrong:**
> "Write a product introduction"

**Right:**
> "Please write in this style: [example]... Requirements: emphasize emotional value, confident tone, under 150 words"

**Why it works:**
Examples help AI precisely understand your expected format, tone, and structure.

## 4. Constraint Method: Draw a Circle for AI

**Wrong:**
> "Recommend some good AI tools"

**Right:**
> "Recommend 5 AI tools for designers that: 1) have free version 2) support Chinese 3) work online..."

**Why it works:**
Constraints make AI output more focused and structured. Without constraints, responses are often generic.

## 5. Iterative Questioning: Don't Settle for First Response

**Wrong:**
> Ask AI once, get answer, end

**Right:**
> Round 1: What is private domain traffic?
> Round 2: What is the difference between private and public domain?
> Round 3: How to build private domain for a淘宝 shop?
> Round 4: Low-budget tips for small businesses?

**Why it works:**
AI's first round is overview-level. The more you ask, the more detailed and specific the answers.

## Practical Case: How I Use These 5 Techniques

Last week I needed to write an "AI tools comparison" article. Here's how I did it:

**Step 1 Role**
> "You are a tech reviewer who has written 50+ AI tool reviews..."

**Step 2 Break it down**
> "Four parts: market overview, feature comparison, user analysis, buying advice"

**Step 3 Add constraints**
> "Each tool: 200 words, include: pros, cons, target users, free/paid"

**Step 4 Iterate**
> "The table in part 2 is messy, please reorganize"

**Step 5 Follow up**
> "Add 3 tips for beginners"

The final article turned out great. This is the power of proper questioning.

## Common Mistakes Summary

| Mistake Type | Problem | Solution |
|--------------|---------|----------|
| Too broad | "Help me write something" | Be specific |
| No role | No AI identity set | Use role-playing |
| Too much at once | Ask everything at once | Split tasks + iterate |
| No constraints | No format/length requirements | Add constraints |
| No examples | Don't know what style you want | Use example guidance |

## Advanced Tips: Prompt Template Library

Here are my most frequently used templates:

**Universal Question Template:**
```
You are [role]. Please [task], meeting these conditions:
1. [condition 1]
2. [condition 2]
3. [condition 3]

Reference: [example]
```

**Analysis Template:**
```
Analyze [topic] from these dimensions:
- Dimension 1: [question]
- Dimension 2: [question]
- Dimension 3: [question]

Requirements: [format/length/style]
```

**Writing Template:**
```
Write [content type], topic is [topic].
- Target readers: [who]
- Expected style: [style]
- Word count: [number]
- Must include: [elements]
```

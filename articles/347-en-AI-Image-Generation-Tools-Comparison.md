## 1. Midjourney - The Name for Artistic Sense

**Pros**:
- Images have top-notch artistic sense and great aesthetics
- Strong prompt understanding, decent images even with simple prompts
- Active community, can reference others' prompts
- Each generation is like opening a blind box, full of surprises

**Cons**:
- Requires VPN in China
- Very limited free tier
- Takes time to learn parameters

**Best for**: Art creation, social media images, concept design

**Price**: Limited free trial, Standard plan $30/month

## 2. DALL-E - The Reliable Veteran

**Pros**:
- Top-notch stability, rarely fails
- Backed by OpenAI, guaranteed quality
- Great at understanding complex prompts
- Can integrate directly with ChatGPT

**Cons**:
- Less artistic than Midjourney
- Requires VPN
- Relatively expensive

**Best for**: Commercial images, educational content, scenarios requiring accuracy

**Price**: Free tier available, Plus $20/month

## 3. Stable Diffusion - The Open Source Powerhouse

**Pros**:
- Completely free, use as you like
- Open source with local deployment, good privacy
- Rich plugins, high customization
- Active community with plenty of models to download

**Cons**:
- Requires some technical knowledge
- High PC requirements
- Output quality depends on models and parameters

**Best for**: Tech-savvy users, those needing local deployment, budget-conscious users

**Price**: Free (requires hardware investment)

## 4. Tongyi Wanxiang - Domestic Rising Star

**Pros**:
- No VPN needed, accessible in China
- Good understanding of Chinese prompts
- Fast generation speed
- Completely free

**Cons**:
- Artistic sense lags behind Midjourney
- Relatively limited styles
- Detail processing needs improvement

**Best for**: Chinese users, everyday images, simple needs

**Price**: Completely free

## Pro Tips: Prompts Are Everything

Regardless of which tool you use, **prompts are the key to determining output quality**.

Here are some foolproof prompt tips:

1. **Be specific about style**: e.g., "cinematic lighting", "oil painting style", "photorealistic"
2. **Describe composition**: add "wide shot", "close-up", "bird's eye view"
3. **Be detailed about specifics**: describe materials, colors, lighting like "golden hour lighting, soft shadows"
4. **Use negative prompts**: use "--no" to exclude unwanted elements like "--no text, --no watermark"

# How to do breadcrumb navigation? Don’t just focus on placing a row of links, these 5 details are more effective for SEO

## Why do many websites clearly have breadcrumb navigation, but there is almost no sense of SEO?

I've seen this happen too many times.

Many people do breadcrumb navigation with a very simple idea:

- Place a row of "Home > Categories > Articles" at the top of the page
- Visually it looks like that
-Users may also be able to click
- Then by default, you have already completed the breadcrumb navigation.

But when it comes to SEO, it often doesn’t play much role.

The reason is not complicated: **Many websites do "look like breadcrumbs" and are not "really helping search engines understand the structure." **

For example, some frequently asked questions include:

- The classification level is messy, hang it here today and hang it there tomorrow.
- The current page appears in several different paths at the same time
- There are breadcrumbs on the page, but no breadcrumb schema in the code
- Hidden directly on the mobile phone, neither users nor search engines can see the logic clearly
- Only the article page has it, but not the column page and product page.

So if you have been studying how to make breadcrumb navigation recently, let me give you a very straightforward conclusion:

**Breadcrumb navigation is not to "look more professional on the page", but to clearly explain the website hierarchy, user paths and internal link relationships. **

Once this is done right, users will find pages more smoothly, and search engines will understand the website structure more stably.

---

## What exactly does breadcrumb navigation help with? Don’t just think of it as a back button

Many people’s understanding of breadcrumb navigation is still limited to “convenient for users to return to the previous level.”

This is certainly true, but this is not the only place where it is truly valuable.

When I look at the breadcrumbs of a website, I will focus on whether it has done these three things well:

### 1. Tell the user: which layer you are on now

Especially for websites with a lot of content and deep categories, users do not come in from the homepage every time.

Many people land directly on a certain article, product page, or topic page from the search results.

At this time, the role of the breadcrumb navigation is to let him know at a glance:

- Which column does this page belong to?
- What else is there on the next level?
- Where can I continue next?

### 2. Tell search engines: Who owns this page in the website structure?

This is even more important for SEO.

A clear breadcrumb path actually tells search engines:

> This page is not an isolated piece of content, it has a clear ownership in the entire website.

The clearer your website hierarchy is, the easier it will be for search engines to understand:

- What are the upper columns?
- What are the specific content pages?
- Which pages have a parent-child relationship?

### 3. Create a natural internal link relationship

Breadcrumbs are essentially internal links.

Moreover, it is not the kind of hard "adding links for the sake of adding links", but a path-type link that is inherently reasonable and users are willing to click on it.

A well-done breadcrumb navigation can not only help users return to the previous level, but also provide some core column pages with more stable internal link support.

## How to use breadcrumb navigation? I would rather suggest getting these 5 details right first.

### 1. Determine the website level first, and then write the breadcrumbs. Don’t write them as you go.

The biggest problem with breadcrumbs on many websites is not that the style is not good-looking, but that the access path itself is unstable. **

For example, articles of the same type:

- Today I am hanging under "SEO Tutorial"
- Tomorrow it will be listed under “Content Marketing”
- The day after tomorrow, in order to collect keywords, add another layer of "Webmaster Academy"

The result of this is that the breadcrumbs look busy, but no one can actually figure out where this page belongs.

So my habits have always been simple:

**Determine the website level first, and then let the breadcrumbs truly reflect this level. **

For example, if an article is long-term, it belongs to:

Home > SEO Optimization > Technical SEO > Article Details

Then don’t change it to one thing today and another thing tomorrow.

Stable paths are more important than fancy paths.

---

### 2. You must be able to click on the previous level, and do not link back to yourself on the current page.

A small detail that is practical but easily overlooked:

**The real value in breadcrumbs is the previous hierarchical links, not the last current page. **

So I usually handle it like this:

- Home page is clickable
- The category page is clickable
-Clickable subcategory pages
- The current page will only be displayed, no self-links will be added

The reason is simple.

When users click on breadcrumbs, they usually want to go up, not refresh the current page.

If the last item is also made clickable, there will be no benefit in terms of experience, and the amount of information will not be increased in terms of structure.

There is one more thing not to ignore:

**Don’t cram in too many layers just to look rich. **

If your path is so long that users don’t want to finish it, then its navigation value is already declining.

---

### 3. The breadcrumb path should be aligned with the URL, title, and canonical as much as possible

This step is the easiest for many people to do.

I've seen some pages that ostensibly have breadcrumb navigation, but the background signals are fighting:

- Breadcrumbs say this article belongs to Category A
- The URL is Category B
- The page title emphasizes the C theme
- canonical points to another version

In this case, what the search engine receives is not "clear structure", but "you didn't even think about it yourself".

So a more stable approach is:

- A page should have one main attribution as much as possible
- The breadcrumb path should be consistent with the actual level of the page as much as possible
- URL, column, canonical don’t talk about each other

Especially content sites are most afraid of "letting an article fall into several paths at the same time in order to occupy a few more words."

In the short term, it looks like paving more roads, but in the long term, it is easier to mess up the structure.

---

### 4. Not only article pages need breadcrumbs, but deep column pages and product pages should also have them.

Many websites will make a very typical problem:

**Breadcrumbs are only placed on the article details page, and other in-depth pages are left to the user’s own guessing. **

But from the perspective of user path and website structure optimization, the following pages are actually very suitable to add:

- Secondary column page
-Level 3 topic page
- Product details page
- Help center details page
- Documentation page

Especially for sites with many products, deep categories, and large amounts of content, users are more likely to get lost the further they go.

At this time, breadcrumb navigation is not a dispensable decoration, but an important path to help users continue browsing.

Another experience is:

**Don’t just hide it on mobile if it’s too much trouble. **

You can simplify the style, but don’t remove it completely.

A lot of real traffic comes from the mobile side. If there is no clear breadcrumb navigation on the mobile side, it will be more difficult for users to go back to the previous level, and there will be one less structural signal.

---

### 5. It is not enough to see it on the page, it is best to add breadcrumb schema

This is a very critical step in technical SEO.

After creating the breadcrumb navigation, many people think that the task is over.

In fact, there is still one step left: **tell the search engine that this string of links is the breadcrumb path. **

The most common way is to add structured data like `BreadcrumbList`.

Its meaning is:

- Help search engines understand hierarchical relationships more clearly
- Improve the probability that breadcrumb signals are correctly recognized
- Opportunity to show clearer paths in search results

So if you are only doing front-end display and haven’t done breadcrumb schema yet, I would recommend you to make it up.

Don’t rush to complete it after you finish it. It’s best to check again:

- Is the hierarchical order consistent with the page display?
- Is the link address an official URL?
- Have you marked the current page position correctly?
- Are there any structural errors reported in the test tool?

Don’t let “it looks like it’s on the page” turn into “the actual search engine doesn’t recognize it.”

## Which pages do I usually not force breadcrumb?

Not all pages have to do this either.

Scenarios that I generally wouldn’t recommend include the following:

- Single conversion landing page
- Very lightly structured one-page page
- Temporary event page
- A page with a very single goal and almost no need to continue browsing

Because this type of page does not rely on hierarchical browsing to survive.

But as long as the page is in a relatively clear website structure, especially the content page, product page, and documentation page, I will basically recommend making a serious version.

---

## If breadcrumb navigation has been added, but there is still no improvement in SEO, check these 4 things first.

### 1. Do the paths just “look right” but not actually unified?

If the path of the same type of pages keeps changing, no matter how many breadcrumbs there are, it will only be a performative navigation.

### 2. Is this page really caught by the website structure?

If the page itself is like an isolated island, with neither column entrances nor internal links in the text, then a single breadcrumb cannot drive the overall structure signal.

### 3. Is the schema missing or written incorrectly?

Just because the front-end display is normal does not mean that search engines must understand it properly.

### 4. Is the upper column worth strengthening?

If the content of your category page itself is very thin, the titles are messy, and it is not continuously updated, then the breadcrumbs will lead back the weight and path, and the effect will not be particularly ideal.

So don’t just focus on the breadcrumbs themselves, but also look back to see if the upper column pages hold up.

## The last thing to say

How to make breadcrumb navigation? It’s really not complicated.

What it fears most is not that you don’t know how to write that row of links, but that you treat it as a pure style component and forget about it after you finish it.

A truly useful breadcrumb navigation should do three things at the same time:

- Users know where they are at a glance
- Search engines can understand the page ownership at a glance
- The relationship between the internal hierarchy and internal links of the website is smoother

If you happen to be organizing your website recently, you might as well pull out the breadcrumb navigation and check it out.

Many websites don’t have enough content, but the structure and signals are too messy.

Breadcrumbs are often those details that may seem inconspicuous but are worth adding later.

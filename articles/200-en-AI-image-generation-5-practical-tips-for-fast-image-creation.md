## Pain point 1: The description words are not written well, and the generated pictures are completely wrong.

When many people first started using AI drawing, they wrote the descriptors very casually:
"Draw me a nice picture" "Generate a nice logo"

The resulting pictures often look different.

**What's the problem? ** AI is not a roundworm in your stomach, it doesn’t know what is thinking in your mind.

My approach: **Structured Description = Subject + Scene + Style + Details**

> Example:
> ❌ "Draw a city map"
> ✅ "Futuristic technology-style city night scene, cyberpunk neon light effect, tall buildings, flying cars in the sky, mainly blue and purple tones, movie-level picture quality"

> Example:
> ❌ "Generate a logo"
> ✅ "Minimalist style technology company logo, deformation of the letter 'A', gradient blue tone, with glowing effect, suitable as an App icon"

**Core Skill**: Turn the vague "good-looking" into a specific visual description.

## Pain point 2: The style is always poorly grasped

With the same theme, the pictures generated by others are full of high-end feeling, while the pictures you generated are rustic.

The problem is most likely with the **style keyword**.

My method: **Remember a few universal style combinations**

| Desired feeling | Recommended style words |
|----------|-------------|
| Technological sense | cyberpunk, futuristic, neon, holographic |
| minimalist, clean, flat design |
| Cinematic, 8k, photorealistic, professional photography |
| Illustration | illustration, digital art, vector style |
| vintage, retro, 1980s style |

> Actual measurement is valid:
> Once I helped a friend create a promotional image for a coffee shop. After adding "warm lighting, cozy atmosphere, film grain", the quality of the entire image was improved by more than one level.
> My friend thought it was taken by a professional photographer.

## Pain point 3: The resolution of the generated image is not enough

If you use the images generated by AI directly, you often encounter a problem - the resolution is not enough and it becomes blurry when you zoom in.

Especially when making posters and PPTs, high resolution is particularly important.

My method: **Use HD Repair function**

Mainstream AI drawing tools all have HD repair or Upscale functions:
- Midjourney: use --upbeta or --testp parameters
- Stable Diffusion: Use amplification models such as RealESRGAN
- DALL·E 3: Directly supports generating 1024x1024

> Tips:
> For 1024x1024 images, the resolution is still sufficient after being enlarged 4 times.
> When making an e-commerce main image, the width must be at least 2000px.

## Pain point 4: How to calculate copyright issues?

Many people are worried: Can images generated by AI be used commercially? Will it be infringement?

This is a good question, the current situation is this:

**About Copyright:**
- At present, the law is not yet completely clear on the copyright ownership of AI-generated content.
- But generally speaking: there is no problem with commercial use of images generated by AI.
- If you are particularly worried, you can make secondary modifications to the AI-generated pictures to add your "original components"

**About the risk of infringement:**
- Try to avoid directly imitating the style of a specific artist
- Do not use well-known IP, trademarks, or celebrity portraits (these have legal risks)
- It is recommended to consult professional legal advice before commercial use

> My approach:
> I will make secondary adjustments to the pictures generated by AI, such as adding my own logo, color adjustment, secondary composition, etc.
> This not only retains the efficiency of AI, but also increases originality, making it more secure to use.

## My AI drawing workflow

I am making a picture now, which usually looks like this:

1. **Clear requirements** → What kind of pictures are needed and where to use them?
2. **Write descriptive words** → Use a structured method, subject + scene + style + details
3. **Generate first draft** → Generate 4 pictures at a time, choose the most satisfactory one
4. **HD Repair** → Zoom in to the required resolution
5. **Post-adjustment** → Color adjustment, adding elements, and secondary composition

The entire process, from demand confirmation to finished product, usually takes 10-15 minutes.

In the past, it would take half a day to ask a designer to make a picture, but now AI can do it in a few minutes.

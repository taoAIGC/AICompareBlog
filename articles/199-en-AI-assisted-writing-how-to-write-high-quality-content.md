#AI-Assisted Writing: How to use AI to write high-quality content

> Language: Chinese | Region: Mainland China | Keywords: AI writing, AI creation, AI-assisted writing, AI article writing, AI content generation

---

## written in front

Recently, many friends have asked me: "I want to write something, but I always get stuck and can't write it. What should I do?"

I said: "Have you tried AI?"

Their first reaction is: "Can the things written by AI be used?" "Will it feel very mechanical?"

Actually, AI writing is not about letting AI think and write for you - it makes you think faster and write more smoothly.

Today I will share how I use AI to assist writing. It’s not high-tech, just a few practical tips.

---

## Pain point 1: I get stuck while writing.

I believe many people have this situation: they open a document and hold it in for half an hour before writing the first sentence.

The reason is simple: **The most difficult thing about writing is not writing, but thinking about "what to write next." **

My method: let AI help me "continue writing" or "expand".

> "I just wrote [specific content], help me think about 3 directions"
> "In what ways can I develop this point of view?"
> "Give me 3 examples to illustrate this point"

AI does not help you write, but helps you open up your ideas. The direction it gives may not be completely correct, but it can trigger your inspiration.

> My real experience:
> I once wrote an article about time management and got stuck on the section "Why many people don't manage time well".
> I asked AI to help me analyze 5 possible reasons, selected 2 that I agreed with, and made slight modifications to become the core point of the article.
> If I thought about it by myself, I would probably be stuck all afternoon.

---

## Pain point 2: Halfway through writing, I found that there are not enough examples.

The biggest fear when writing content is that you have an idea but can’t think of examples.

Even after holding it back for a long time to come up with an example, I felt forced.

My method: **Ask AI directly for examples. **

> "I want to explain [XX point of view], can you give me 3 examples from life?"
> "Regarding [XX topic], what are some classic cases?"
> "Explain the concept with easy-to-understand examples"

AI has a large knowledge base and can give you a bunch of examples in a few seconds. You just need to screen and verify which one is the most suitable.

> Actual measurement is valid:
> Once I was writing an article on "First Principles" and needed an example from life to illustrate "thinking back to the essence".
> I asked AI to give 5 examples. I chose "Buying milk tea - some people buy it directly, and some people ask themselves whether they really need it first." This one is very appropriate.
> Readers have reported that this example is particularly easy to understand.

---

## Pain point 3: I can’t see the problem after writing it

After writing an article, I always feel that something is strange, but I can’t put it clearly.

Don’t hold on at this time and let AI help you “review” it.

> "Please help me see if this article is logically logical?"
> "Isn't this paragraph a bit wordy?"
> "What do I want to express in this paragraph? Can you help me simplify it?"

AI will not be tired or embarrassed to tell the truth. It can help you discover:
- where logic jumps
- A place for wordy expressions
- Insufficient examples

> My habits:
> After writing the first draft, be sure to let AI go over it. It often points out places where I "think I wrote clearly but actually didn't."
> This is much more effective than reading your own articles - because when you read what you have written, you will always make supplements in your head.

---

## Pain point 4: Don’t know how to choose the title

Many people have finished writing their articles and spent half an hour thinking about the title.

My method: **Let AI help you choose the title. **

> "Help me come up with 10 titles for this article that will attract people to click on it."
> "What are the rules for popular titles on this topic?"
> "Give these keywords, generate several different styles of titles"

AI can give you dozens of options in seconds. All you have to do is pick and tweak.

> Real case:
> Once I wrote an article about AI learning, which gave AI 20 titles.
> I chose "AI-assisted learning: 5 ways to learn faster", which later became the most read article that week.
> I certainly can't think of such a "clickbait" that's not exaggerated.

---

## Pain point 5: I don’t want to write in the middle of writing

This is the most common thing - I lose motivation as I write.

My method: **Let AI help me break down tasks. **

> "Help me break this 3,000-word article into several parts."
> "From what angle would it be easier to write about this topic?"
> "Which part is easier to write first?"

Break down big tasks into smaller tasks and the stress will be less.

> My tips:
> Now when writing long articles, I will first let AI help me outline the outline. After confirming that there are no problems, I will write it paragraph by paragraph.
>Write only a small portion at a time and it will be less easy to give up.

---

## My AI writing process

When I write now, it usually goes like this:

1. **Stuck in topic selection** → Let AI help me Brainstorm
2. **Make an outline** → Let AI help me sort out the structure
3. **Write the first draft** → When I get stuck, let AI help me continue writing or add examples.
4. **Modification** → Let AI help me check logic and expression
5. **Start title** → Let AI generate multiple options for me

In the whole process, AI is not the leader, but **my writing accelerator**.

---

## Write at the end

AI writing is not magic. It does not mean that AI will give you a perfect article if you just give it a topic.

It's more like a **super assistant** - helping you think when you think slowly, giving you inspiration when you can't write, and pointing out your problems for you when you can't see clearly.

**But you still have to come up with the core ideas and opinions yourself. **

With good use of AI, writing efficiency can really be doubled. If you don’t believe it, you can try it.

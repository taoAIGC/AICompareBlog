## Detailed Comparison

### 1. remove.bg - The Industry Veteran

remove.bg is one of the earliest AI background removal products, launched in 2018.

**Pros**:
- Stable results, especially for people and products
- Supports API calls for batch processing
- Supports 50+ languages including Chinese
- Has Photoshop plugin
- Compatible with major design software

**Cons**:
- Free version only previews with watermark
- Paid version is pricey ($0.05/piece)
- May need to queue during peak times
- Complex backgrounds sometimes not handled well

**Best for**: E-commerce sellers, designers, businesses needing API batch processing

**My experience**: remove.bg is a "fail-safe" choice. Most images turn out pretty good, rarely with edge serrations or missing hair.

I tested a product photo and a portrait. The product photo was clean, even handling reflective edges well. The portrait was slightly worse, with some hair粘连, but overall acceptable.

The free version forces watermarks. For formal use, you need to pay. For bulk purchases, you can negotiate down to a few cents per piece, but still costly for large volumes.

Clipdrop is from Stability AI, backed by open source models, with especially comprehensive features.

**Cons**:
- Too many features make the interface cluttered

- Background removal results not as stable as professional tools

**Best for**: Designers, content creators, users needing one-stop solutions

**My experience**: Clipdrop is not just a simple background removal tool, but an "all-in-one" AI image processing suite. Besides background removal, it also has:
- Stable Diffusion XL upscaling
- AI object removal
- One-click background replacement
- Lighting adjustments

These features cost extra individually, but Clipdrop bundles them together at a reasonable price.

However, if your need is just "simple background removal," Clipdrop might be overkill. Its background removal is average, not as stable as remove.bg, not as cheap as Pixian AI.

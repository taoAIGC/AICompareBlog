## Detailed Comparison

### 1. Zoom - The Reliable Veteran

Zoom is the veteran in video conferencing, with stability as its core advantage.

**Pros**:
- Clear and stable video quality, excellent network adaptability
- Globally universal, top choice for international meetings
- Rich features: breakout rooms, virtual backgrounds, recording, etc.
- Rich ecosystem, great compatibility

**Cons**:
- Requires VPN in China
- Free version has time limits (40 minutes)
- Lacks domestic ecosystem integration

**Best For**: People who need international meetings, expat employees, cross-border business

**My Experience**: Zoom's biggest advantage is stability. No matter how bad the network, it can maintain connection and video quality won't suffer too much. When having international meetings with Zoom, I've never had issues. But using it in China is inconvenient - you need a VPN for stable connections, and the free version's 40-minute limit is annoying for long meetings.

---

### 2. Tencent Meeting - The Best Domestic Experience

Tencent Meeting has the highest market share in China with a very smooth experience.

**Pros**:
- Best network optimization in China, never lags
- Join meetings with one click via WeChat/WeCom
- Cloud recording, convenient and efficient
- Practical subtitle and transcription features

**Cons**:
- International experience is average, not recommended for cross-border
- Relatively closed ecosystem
- Advanced features require payment

**Best For**: Domestic enterprises, teams with frequent meetings, users needing local experience

**Use Experience**: In China, Tencent Meeting is the definition of "smooth." Just open it, enter the verification code, and join - almost no learning curve. The integration with WeChat and WeCom is great; clients can click a link and join directly without downloading software. But if you need to meet with overseas colleagues, you'll need to switch to Zoom.

---

### 3. Feishu - The All-in-One Collaboration Tool

Feishu is not just a meeting tool, but a complete collaborative office suite.

**Pros**:
- Integrated documents, approvals, calendars, and meetings
- Powerful meeting assistant - automatically generates meeting notes
- Real-time translation and subtitles work well
- Integrated with ByteDance ecosystem

**Cons**:
- Slightly higher learning curve, too many features
- Non-ByteDance companies may not adapt well
- Meeting stability and professionalism not as good as Zoom

**Best For**: Internet teams, teams pursuing efficient collaboration, ByteDance employees

**Use Experience**: Feishu's meeting function is part of its entire collaboration ecosystem. You can share documents before the meeting, it automatically generates notes after, and can turn items into tasks with one click. This workflow is very smooth, especially for internet teams. But if you just need a simple meeting tool, Feishu might be overkill.

---

### 4. DingTalk - The All-Purpose Enterprise Manager

DingTalk is Alibaba's enterprise communication tool with comprehensive features and strong management attributes.

**Pros**:
- Complete enterprise management features (attendance, approvals, workflows)
- Powerful group meetings and video meetings
- Message delivery confirmation with read receipts
- Rich ecosystem, integrated with many enterprise services

**Cons**:
- Interface is quite "enterprise," not simple enough
- Not very friendly for individual users
- Meeting experience is average

**Best For**: Traditional enterprises, teams needing strong management, those with Alibaba ecosystem needs

**Use Experience**: DingTalk is more designed for enterprise managers. The "read" feature puts pressure on employees, but it does improve execution. Video conferencing has all the necessary features, though nothing particularly outstanding. If your company already uses DingTalk for attendance and approvals, the meeting feature as an extension is still quite useful.

---

### 5. Microsoft Teams - The Microsoft Ecosystem Favorite

Microsoft Teams is Microsoft's team collaboration tool, deeply integrated with Office 365.

**Pros**:
- Seamless integration with Word, Excel, PowerPoint
- Enterprise-level security and compliance
- Channels and tags feature suitable for team collaboration
- High internationalization

**Cons**:
- Unstable access in China
- Steep learning curve, too complex
- Meeting quality average, sometimes lags

**Best For**: Microsoft enterprises, teams using Office 365, foreign companies

**Use Experience**: Teams' advantage is in the "ecosystem." If you're already using Outlook, SharePoint, Office 365, then Teams is a natural extension. But in pure meeting experience, Teams is not as good as Zoom and Tencent Meeting. Suitable for teams that need to frequently share Office documents and collaborate deeply.

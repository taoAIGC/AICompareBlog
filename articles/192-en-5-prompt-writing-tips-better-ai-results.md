# 5 Prompt Writing Tips to Get Better Results from AI

> Language: English | Region: Global | Keywords: AI Prompts, Prompt Engineering, ChatGPT Tips, Claude Tips, AI Writing Tips

## Why Does Your AI Always Give Irrelevant Answers?

Do you often encounter this situation: you ask AI a question, but its answers are either too vague or completely off-topic?

Don't blame the AI just yet - chances are your **prompt wasn't written correctly**.

As someone who works with AI every day, I've made more mistakes than anyone. Today, I'm sharing 5 top prompt writing tips that will definitely improve your AI response quality by several levels.

---

## 1. Role Assignment: Give It an Identity First

**Core Technique:** Tell AI who it is.

### Wrong Way
> "Help me write a work summary"

### Right Way
> "You are a senior product manager with 10 years of experience in the internet industry. Please help me write a quarterly work summary, focusing on user growth achievements"

**Effect:** With role assignment, AI automatically responds from a professional perspective, with deeper content that fits actual work scenarios.

---

## 2. Background Information: Make the Context Clear

**Core Technique:** Give AI enough context.

### Wrong Way
> "This plan doesn't work, how do I fix it?"

### Right Way
> "I'm building a social app for college students. Currently we have 5,000 daily active users, and our goal is to grow to 20,000 in three months. Please provide 5 specific growth strategies for this goal"

**Effect:** Without context, AI can only guess. With context, it can give truly useful advice.

---

## 3. Output Format: Tell It What Form You Want

**Core Technique:** Clearly specify the output format.

### Wrong Way
> "Recommend some English learning methods"

### Right Way
> "Please list 10 English learning methods and their suitable audiences in table format, including method name, daily time investment, and expected results"

**Effect:** After specifying the format, AI output is well-structured and clear - no need to reorganize yourself.

---

## 4. Constraints: Set Boundaries Upfront

**Core Technique:** Clearly tell AI what to avoid.

### Wrong Way
> "Help me write a product description"

### Right Way
> "Write a 500-word headphone product description focusing on sound quality and noise cancellation advantages. Don't mention price, avoid technical terms, target ordinary consumers"

**Effect:** With constraints, AI won't go off on tangents - it follows your requirements strictly.

---

## 5. Step-by-Step Guidance: Don't Rush Everything

**Core Technique:** Break large tasks into smaller ones.

### Wrong Way
> "Help me write a complete business plan"

### Right Way:
> "Step 1: Please help me analyze the current state and trends of the wireless power bank market
> Step 2: Please identify main competitors and their strengths/weaknesses
> Step 3: Based on the above analysis, propose 3 feasible product differentiation directions"

**Effect:** Going step by step, AI responds precisely to each step. Having it integrate later produces much better results than one-shot generation.

---

## Practical Exercise: How to Craft a Top-Tier Prompt

Let me demonstrate how to optimize a regular request into an effective prompt:

### Original Version
> "Help me write an article about AI"

### Optimized Version
> "You are a tech columnist who excels at explaining complex concepts in plain language. Please help me write a 1500-word article explaining what AI is and how it's changing our lives. Requirements: 1) Use 3 real-life examples to illustrate AI applications 2) Language should be vivid and interesting for general readers 3) Finally give 3 suggestions for ordinary people to start using AI. Avoid technical terms and don't mention any specific AI products."

**Comparison:** The optimized prompt has clear role, specific goals, format specification, and constraints - AI can't help but give a great answer.

## Final Thoughts

Good prompts are like good questions - people who ask well always get better answers.

These 5 techniques don't require memorizing any programming language. All you need is **spending 10 extra seconds to provide clear context before making any request**.

Try it - follow this framework next time you ask AI, and the results will definitely be different.

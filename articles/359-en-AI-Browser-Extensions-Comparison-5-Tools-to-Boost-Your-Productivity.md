## 1. Sider: The Jack-of-All-Trades

**My Experience:**

Sider is my main plugin. It integrates top-tier AIs like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini directly into the browser sidebar.

Most common uses:
- Select text and have it summarized, translated, or explained
- Open any webpage and have it analyzed
- Have it polish my emails when writing

**Best For:**
- Researchers who frequently look up information
- Content creators who write regularly
- People who like operating directly on web pages

**Weaknesses:**
- Too many features, can be overwhelming for beginners
- Free quota is limited
- Some advanced features require payment

**My Take:** If you're only installing one AI plugin, Sider is the most worry-free choice. It's like an AI assistant that's always on standby—no need to switch windows, you can use it directly on web pages.

---

## 2. Monica: The Dream Plugin for Writers

**My Experience:**

Monica's strongest point is **marketing copy capability**. Give it a product description, and it can generate Xiaohongsu posts, Twitter threads, email body text, ad copy...

I used to spend half an hour writing product introductions. With Monica, I finish the first draft in 5 minutes.

It also has a one-click optimization feature that makes your writing more natural and human.

**Best For:**
- Content creators
- E-commerce operators
- Marketing professionals

**Weaknesses:**
- Free version only gives 20 conversations per day
- Some advanced templates require subscription
- Chinese optimization not as good as English

**My Take:** If you write copy regularly, Monica is a game-changer. But the free version really isn't enough. Given how much time it saves, the paid version is worth it.

---

## 3. WebPilot: A Blessing for Reading Disabilities

**My Experience:**

WebPilot solves a very practical problem: **helping me quickly read through a webpage**.

You just click once, and it summarizes the page content into a few paragraphs, with key points crystal clear. Especially for long, water-down articles, it saves half my time.

It also has a "rewrite" function that explains complex content in plain language—super friendly for someone like me who gets headaches from jargon.

**Best For:**
- People who need to read a lot of industry news
- Students researching
- Anyone who thinks web pages are too long to read

**Weaknesses:**
- Summarization quality is inconsistent
- Occasionally fails to parse certain pages
- Free version has usage limits

**My Take:** If you're often put off by long articles, WebPilot is a must-have. It might not completely replace deep reading, but it's definitely your best friend for filtering information.

---

## 4. Timely: The Time Management Master Hidden in Your Browser

**My Experience:**

Timely is special—it's not for writing, it's for **managing your time**.

It automatically tracks how much time you spend on each tab, then generates visual reports. You can clearly see which website wastes most of your time.

Even smarter: it can automatically set task reminders based on your browsing habits. For example, if you often check email at 10 AM, it'll remind you it's time to check email.

**Best For:**
- People with poor self-discipline
- People who need to understand their time distribution
- People who want to optimize their workflow

**Weaknesses:**
- Interface is somewhat complex
- Niche, few tutorials available
- Chrome only for now

**My Take:** Timely is for people who want to seriously manage their time. It's not a tool that shows immediate results, but over time, you'll gain a whole new perspective on your time.

---

## 5. SaveDay: Your Second Brain

**My Experience:**

SaveDay = Read Later + AI Summarization + Knowledge Management.

When you find an interesting page, click to save—it automatically summarizes, archives it. When you need it, just search by keyword and all related info comes up.

I use it to collect industry news and inspiration materials. When I need to write something, a search brings up all the relevant materials.

**Best For:**
- Content creators who need to collect materials
- People who love "read later"
- People who want to build a personal knowledge base

**Weaknesses:**
- Free version only gives 50 AI summaries per month
- Requires some organization habits
- Chinese content recognition sometimes inaccurate

**My Take:** SaveDay is for "long-termists." It won't show immediate effects, but if you stick with it, you'll build a private knowledge base that's worth more and more over time.

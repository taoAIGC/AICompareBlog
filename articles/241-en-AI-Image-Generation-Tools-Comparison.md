## Detailed comparison

### 1. Midjourney – an art party favorite

Midjourney can be said to be the "top leader" in the field of AI pictures. It started from Discord and has gradually become an industry benchmark.

**advantage**:
- The most artistic sense, the generated pictures are very "textured"
- Active community, lots of prompt references
- Continuously updated, the version is iterated from V1 to V6
- Strong ability to control style
- Suitable for generating abstract and artistic pictures

**shortcoming**:
- Need to access the Internet scientifically
- The learning curve is slightly steeper and there are many parameters
- Very few free credits
- General support for Chinese

**Who is it suitable for**: Designers, art creators, bloggers who pursue visual effects

**My experience of using it**: The effect of Midjourney is really strong. Especially for the V6 version, the images generated have reached commercial level.

I used it to generate several product images:
- "Minimalist style watch advertisement" - light, shadow and materials are all in place
- "Cyberpunk City Night Scene" - an explosion of details and a full sense of atmosphere
- "Retro style poster" - the color tone and font are very correct

But there are also problems:
1. Need to circumvent the wall - high usage cost for domestic users
2. The parameters are complex—--ar, --stylize, --chaos requires learning a bunch of parameters.
3. Strong randomness - the same prompt may produce much different results each time

If you pursue artistic effects and have the ability to circumvent the wall, Midjourney is currently the best choice.

---

### 2. DALL-E 3——King of Understanding

DALL-E is the biological son of OpenAI. It has evolved from DALL-E 2 to DALL-E 3, and its understanding ability has made a qualitative leap.

**advantage**:
- The most accurate understanding of prompt words
- Fast generation
- Security filters are relatively reasonable
- Can be used directly in ChatGPT
- Text rendering capabilities have been greatly improved

**shortcoming**:
- Not as artistic as Midjourney
- The price is relatively high
- Requires ChatGPT Plus subscription
- Limited space for customization

**Who is it suitable for**: Users who need precise control over content, deep users of ChatGPT

**My experience of using it**: The biggest advantage of DALL-E 3 is that "it understands what you say".

Tried several complex prompts:
- "A girl in a red dress sits by the window of a cafe, with a view of Paris street view" - perfect restoration
- "Generate an APP icon for me, simple style, with a moon pattern in the upper left corner" - accurate text rendering
- "A photo of food with 'Delicious' written on it in cursive letters" - the text is finally no longer garbled

Compared with the previous generation, the text rendering capability of DALL-E 3 has been greatly improved, which is the biggest surprise.

The disadvantage is that the artistic style is relatively "standardized" and lacks the spiritual feeling of Midjourney.

---

### 3. Stable Diffusion——The King of Open Source Freedom

Stable Diffusion is an AI image generation tool that is truly "available to everyone" - open source, free and locally runnable.

**advantage**:
- Completely free (local deployment)
- Rich open source ecosystem with numerous models and plug-ins
- Can be run locally to protect privacy
- ControlNet is powerful
- No usage restrictions at all

**shortcoming**:
- High hardware requirements (needs a good graphics card)
- Difficult to get started and requires configuration
- The interface is not as friendly as commercial products
- Quality issues vary

**Who is it suitable for**: Technology enthusiasts, users who want complete control, businesses that need batch generation

**My experience**: Stable Diffusion is a typical example of "high threshold but high returns".

The configuration I use myself:
- RTX 4080 graphics card
- Automatic1111 WebUI
- Installed common plug-ins such as ControlNet and LoRA

The generation efficiency is pretty good:
- Generate product pictures in batches - 4 pictures in 1 minute
- Training exclusive LoRA - completed in 3 hours
- ControlNet control posture - hit wherever you point

But the problem is:
1. High investment in graphics cards - good graphics cards are not cheap
2. The learning cost is high - you need to learn prompts, parameters, and plug-ins.
3. Maintenance trouble - drivers and models often need to be updated

If you are willing to invest the time and hardware, Stable Diffusion offers a level of flexibility that other tools cannot offer.

---

### 4. Leonardo AI – a feature-rich rising star

Leonardo AI is a new player that has emerged in the past two years. It has quickly emerged in the industry with its rich functions and active community.

**advantage**:
- Generous free quota (150 tokens per day)
- Rich functions (real-time generation, Canvas editing, etc.)
- The community is active and prompts are shared a lot
- Many models to choose from
- Support Chinese interface

**shortcoming**:
- The quality of some model generation is unstable
- The server sometimes freezes
- Premium features require payment
- Still slightly inferior to Midjourney

**Who is it suitable for**: Newbies who want free quota, users who like to explore different styles

**My experience of using it**: Leonardo AI is "a good friend of entry-level players".

The free quota is really great:
- 150 tokens per day is enough for a novice to play for a few days
- All basic functions are free
- The community has a large number of prompt references

Tried several functions:
- "Real-time generation" - watching the pictures change little by little, it's healing
- "Canvas Edit" - partial redrawing is very convenient
- "Pre-trained model" - various styles, including animation and realism

The disadvantage is that some models are of average quality and it takes a lot of trying to find a good one.

---

### 5. Adobe Firefly—a new toy for design veterans

Adobe Firefly is an AI image generation tool from the Adobe family, focusing on "seamless integration".

**advantage**:
- Perfectly integrated with Adobe Family Bucket
- Can be used directly in PS and AI
- Clear business authorization
- Easy to operate, you can get started if you know PS
- The generated pictures can be directly used for commercial purposes

**shortcoming**:
- Functionality is relatively basic
- Not as artistic as Midjourney
- Adobe subscription required
- Average generation speed

**Who is it suitable for**: Designers, users who are already using Adobe Family Bucket, users who need commercial authorization

**My experience of using it**: The greatest value of Firefly is "worry-free".

There is no additional charge (just subscribe to Adobe), and the generated images are available directly:
- AI image enlargement in Photoshop - the effect is quite natural
- Generate vector materials in Illustrator - convenient
- Generate background image - just right for video

But if you pursue artistic effects, Firefly may not satisfy you. It is more suitable for "tool properties" - quick drawings and quick modifications.

## My recommendation

### Recommended by scene

- **Pursue artistic effect**: Midjourney, the strongest effect
- **Pursuing accurate understanding**: DALL-E 3, best understands your needs
- **Pursuit is completely free**: Stable Diffusion, can be run locally
- **Pursuing entry-level friendliness**: Leonardo AI, many free credits
- **Pursuing seamless integration**: Adobe Firefly, the first choice for designers

### Recommended by budget

- **Budget 0**: Stable Diffusion (local) or Leonardo AI (free credit)
- **Budget within 50 yuan**: Leonardo AI paid version
- **Budget over 100 yuan**: Midjourney Professional Edition

### Recommended by user group

- **Professional Designer**: Midjourney + Adobe Firefly Combination
- **Self-media blogger**: Midjourney (visual) + DALL-E 3 (fast)

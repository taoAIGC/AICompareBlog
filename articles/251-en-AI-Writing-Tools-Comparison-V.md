## Detailed Comparison

### 1. ChatGPT - The "All-Rounder" in the AI World

ChatGPT is the pioneer of this AI wave, created by OpenAI and released in late 2022, becoming globally popular instantly.

**Pros**:
- Strongest comprehensive capabilities
- Mature ecosystem
- Rich plugin features
- GPT-4o version has powerful performance
- Supports multimodal (see images, speak)
- Rich community resources
- Complete API interfaces

**Cons**:
- Free version has knowledge cutoff
- Requires VPN
- Sometimes not deep enough in Chinese understanding
- Paid version is pricey ($20/month)
- Sometimes answers are too "correct" and lack soul

**Who it's for**: Users needing comprehensive AI capabilities, developers, content creators

**My experience**: ChatGPT is the "all-rounder" player.

ChatGPT is like a super straight-A student, strong in every aspect. You ask it programming questions, it writes code for you. You ask about writing techniques, it analyzes article structures. You ask it to roleplay, it performs convincingly.

I use ChatGPT most in daily work for: writing code, looking up information, editing copy. Its logical ability is really strong, especially writing code, saving me a lot of time.

The latest GPT-4o supports voice conversation - you can "call" it and discuss problems in real-time. Once you try this feature, you can't go back.

The downside is it requires VPN. And the free version has knowledge cutoff, only paid version can use the latest.

Also, ChatGPT's answers are sometimes too "correct," like a model student's standard answer, lacking some human touch.

---

### 2. Claude - The "Thinker" Who Understands You Best

Claude is an AI assistant launched by Anthropic, featuring "human-like thinking" and called by many "the most human-like AI."

**Pros**:
- Thinking process more like humans
- Strong long text processing
- More natural writing style
- Good at understanding context
- Free version is sufficient
- No need to switch frequently
- Outstanding document analysis

**Cons**:
- Requires VPN
- Knowledge cutoff exists
- Plugin ecosystem not as rich as ChatGPT
- No voice call support
- Weaker multimodal capabilities

**Who it's for**: Users needing deep thinking, writers, long-text workers

**My experience**: Claude is the "thinking" player.

Claude's biggest feature is being "like a person." It doesn't mechanically answer questions but really "thinks" before responding.

My favorite scenarios for using Claude: writing long articles, content planning, analyzing documents. When I ask it to help write an article, it doesn't just pile on content but really understands your needs and helps you organize step by step.

Especially Claude 3.5 Sonnet version, writing ability reached another level. What it writes has more "human flavor," not looking like AI-generated.

The free version quota is also generous - three hours free weekly, enough for most people's daily use.

The downside is plugins aren't as rich as ChatGPT, and no voice dialogue support, lacking some convenience.

---

### 3. Gemini - Google's "Search Expert"

Gemini is Google's AI assistant, biggest advantage is deep integration with Google ecosystem.

**Pros**:
- Deep integration with Google ecosystem
- Strongest search capabilities
- Real-time information access
- Free version has newest knowledge
- Strong multimodal capabilities
- Seamless integration with YouTube and other platforms
- No VPN needed (accessible in China!)

**Cons**:
- Slightly weaker writing abilities
- Sometimes answers are too brief
- Ecosystem not as rich as ChatGPT
- Average value for money on paid version
- Some Chinese scenarios not well understood

**Who it's for**: Users needing real-time information, Google ecosystem users, researchers

**My experience**: Gemini is the "search" player.

Gemini's biggest advantage is speed and freshness. It integrates Google's search capability - you can ask about recent events and it gives you the latest information.

For example, if you want to know the latest tech news or check the latest stock prices, Gemini is more convenient than ChatGPT and Claude.

And it doesn't need VPN! Chinese users can use it directly - this is really great.

I often use Gemini for: looking up information, getting latest news, analyzing YouTube videos. Its integration with Google ecosystem is really tight.

The downside is relatively weak writing ability. When having it write a long article, the quality is not as good as ChatGPT and Claude.
